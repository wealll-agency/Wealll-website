The process of email marketing is very simple and easy to understand. Based on your intent, the process varies. For commercial or selling purposes, emails will be designed in a creative way with compelling content keeping in mind your audience’s interests, and will also have clear CTAs. Promotional emails are generally sent three to five times a week in order to alert the receivers regarding the latest deals and sales. 

Emails can also be sent for the purpose of educating the audience or making them aware of any topic related to your business or in general. Such emails are sent once or twice a week.

The step-by-step procedure of email marketing:

  • Identifying your target market
  • Determine your primary goal 
  • Understand which email marketing type to opt for
  • Use the latest marketing automation tools to analyze audience behaviors 
  • Design emails with highly engaging content
  • Start sending emails
  • Track progress

Business owners use email marketing to acquire new customers and engage with old customers. The four main types of email marketing are:

Newsletter marketing –  Newsletters are mostly used for providing subscribers with knowledge related to your business. Unless you don’t want your newsletters moved to the trash folder, you must create insightful content in your newsletter that will add value to your subscribers. 

Acquisition emails – After sending a newsletter, it’s time to give your attention to those who have subscribed for more such emails. By providing lucrative offers like exclusive first-buyer discounts, you can convert your subscribers to active customers. 

Retention emails – Retention emails are extremely essential for small businesses in order to make sure that your one-time customer gets converted into a regular purchaser.  By asking for feedback and sending festive or occasion special offerings, you can achieve this. 

Promotional emails – This type of email play an important part in driving sales and pushing leads into the conversion funnel. Promotional emails are used to inform your subscribers and customers about a new product or service launch, openings in new locations, or cross-sell goods.
